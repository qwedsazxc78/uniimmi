# Residence Pass-Talent (RP-T)

The Residence Pass-Talent (RP-T) is a 10-year, multiple-entry visa for highly skilled foreign professionals who contribute to Malaysia's economic growth. It is a strategic initiative managed by TalentCorp Malaysia to attract and retain top talent.

---

### Key Benefits

- **10-Year Duration:** Live and work in Malaysia for up to 10 years, with the option to renew.
- **Employment Flexibility:** Change employers without needing to re-apply for a new pass.
- **Spouse & Dependents:**
  - Spouse and children under 18 are also eligible for a Residence Pass.
  - Spouse is permitted to work in Malaysia without needing a separate work pass.
- **Other Dependents:** Parents, parents-in-law, and children over 18 are eligible for a renewable one-year Social Visit Pass.

---

### Main Eligibility Criteria

Applicants must meet several key requirements at the time of application:

1.  **Work History in Malaysia:**
    - Have worked in Malaysia for a minimum of **three (3) consecutive years**.
    - Must hold a valid Employment Pass (EP) with more than 3 months of validity remaining.

2.  **Minimum Salary:**
    - Earn a basic monthly salary of **at least RM 15,000** (excluding allowances or bonuses).

3.  **Tax Records:**
    - Possess a Malaysian income tax file number.
    - Have paid income tax in Malaysia for at least **two (2) years**.

4.  **Education:**
    - Hold a PhD, Master's, Bachelor's Degree, or a Diploma in any field from a recognized institution.
    - Alternatively, a professional/competency certificate from a recognized professional body.

5.  **Work Experience:**
    - Have a minimum of **five (5) years** of total work experience.

---

### General Document Checklist

- Copy of all pages of your passport.
- Updated Resume/CV.
- Copies of all academic certificates.
- Passport-sized photos (light blue background).
- Current employment contract.
- Job description on company letterhead.
- Tax declaration slips (e-BE & EA Forms) for the last 2 years, with payment receipts.
- Last 3 months' salary slips.
- No Objection Letter (NOC) from your current employer.
- Copy of a local contact person's Malaysian ID card (MyKad).
