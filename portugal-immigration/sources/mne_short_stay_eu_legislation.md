EU Legislation

Treaties and Conventions:

Protocol n. o 19 on the Schengen acquis , Treaty of Lisbon, 13 December 2007;

Convention implementing the Schengen Agreement , 22 September 2000 ( PDF );

Protocol integrating the Schengen acquis into EU Law , Treaty of Amsterdam, 2 October 1997;

Schengen Agreement, 14 June 1985 and 19 June 1990

Regulations:

Regulation (EC) n.º 810/2009, of 13 July, and the following amendments – Visa Code ( PDF );

Comission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2024/1415 of 14 March 2024 amending Regulation (EC) No 810/2009 as regards the amount of the visa fees;

Regulation (EU) n.º 2018/1806, of 14 November (consolidated document) - Listing the third countries whose nationals must be in possession of visas when crossing the external borders and those whose nationals are exempt from that requirement (codification of the Regulation (EC) n.° 539/2001 , of 15 March);

Regulation (EU) n. o 2016/679, of 27 April, and following amendments – General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR);

Regulation (EU) n.o 2016/399, of 9 March, and the following amendments (consolidated) – Schengen Border Code;

Regulation (EU) n.º 265/2010, of 25 March and amendment – Movement of persons with a long-stay visa;

Regulation (EC) n.º 767/2008, of 9 July, and the following amendments – VIS Regulation ( PDF ).

Directives:

Directive (EU) n.o 2016/801, of 11 May and following amendments in force– Conditions of entry and residence of third-country nationals for the purposes of research, studies, training, voluntary service, pupil exchange schemes or educational projects and au pairing;

Directive n.º 66/2014/EU, of 15 May (original text) and rectification of 2014 in force– Conditions of entry and residence of third-country nationals in the framework of an intra-corporate transfer;

Directive n.º 36/2014/EU, of 26 February (original text) and rectification of 2014 in force– Seasonal work;

Directive n.o 2004/38/CE, of 29 April and following rectification and amendment in force – Freedom of movement on the right of citizens of the Union and their family members to move and reside freely within the territory of the Member States.
